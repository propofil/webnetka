What free demos are essential on the quest?

Explanations: You have just bought a MetaQuest headset and want to test VR and show it all to family and friends?

So here are the free applications that I recommend to you right away :

First steps with the Quest 2&3

Normally you don't have to download it because it is installed by default. In your headset, open the menu, click on the far right (appLibray), then click on the kind of funnel at the top (the filter) and choose "tutorial". And normally you should find it there.

This free app introduces you to the world of VR, and at the end offers you 2 games: a shooting game, and a small animation with a character who dances with you. Better to do standing up.

Henry

Henry is not a game, but a small cartoon with a lot of charm and not very long. And since it's entirely in VR, you're like inside the cartoon! Very cute.

Elixir

The discovery of this app and its wonderful environment is a very good memory for me. The app will teach you how to do without joysticks (activate manual control in the preferences and above all, then, PLACE the joysticks in a place where they no longer move: the game will be able to start after several long seconds of immobility of the joysticks)

It will also teach you a technique used in almost all quest2 apps (because it reduces motion sickness): teleportation. So usually, you do teleportation with the controllers, but here it explains how to do it with your hands: listen carefully, it will be essential to be able to play this "game".

In this funny app, we're going to do several actions by hand (in the job simulator style), after which we're going to observe some pretty weird phenomena on our hands^^. Do the main part of the game (the one you activate by placing both hands on the sensors on the screen). Then go to free mode (left side) if you want to go back. Magical credits accessible on the right side. Very nice world, fun experience, and very good demo to discover the quest 2.

Stand up. Great for children but they will have to be able to teleport (and not do anything at the risk of bumping themself)

This will trigger an event, then the animal will ask you something else. And as you go, you will unlock 5 short activities (also think about testing all the colors for bogo). Beautiful.

To do standing with a little room around. Very nice for children (but a lot of manipulation to do and be careful not to bump into anything!)

Buying games

All games can be purchased at a reduced price: either while waiting for a discount (new one every week) or through the discount links (25%) given here. Please note that the 2 are not cumulative.

Personally, I only buy on promotions at 30% or 40% (since we already have 25% off with the links, except on certain games under 10€ and except on expansions). And I avoid different game packs that often give you a weak title.



Mirror

You can see what people are seeing in their Quest2 by syncing your smartphone app with your headphones, connecting both to the same wifi network, and clicking "Mirror". A red dot will appear in the player's field of vision.

And the one with the phone can record photos / videos, or connect his/her phone to a big screen (for example with usbC-hdmi cable) to allow everyone to see etc.

Projecting on a computer screen or a smartTV is even easier: from your app in the quest, press menu, mirror, and there either your device is present and you choose it (TV, smartphone app. ..) or you choose computer and launch a web browser at the address "oculus.com/casting" and your image will be displayed there.

Play computer game with MQ2

But you can also play VR games that you possess on your computer (steam VR games for example), by connecting your quest2 by usb cable to your computer. It is then just recognized as an accessory, like a screen in fact, and all the work to run the game will be done by the computer (and for some demanding games, be careful, a good computer will be necessary).

Virtual desktop strongly recommended to improve the resolution, optimize according to games and save preferences per game.

The blur

It is normal to have blur on the sides because there is no auto focus: only the central area is assumed be perfectly clear. So look at this area and adjust:

  • ✔️ the height of your helmet
  • ✔️ its tightening
  • ✔️ the spacing of the 2 eyepieces (spread your eyepieces or bring them closer and test, between the last 2 notches for adults)

    • Watch out for "spots" in the viewing area. They are often due to passages of light on the side of the nose or through the forehead. A suitable face mask removes them. Finally, be careful to protect your lenses from the dust or light, to avoid irreversible stains.
